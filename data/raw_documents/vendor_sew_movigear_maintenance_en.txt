===================================================
LUBRICATION SCHEDULE
===================================================

Recommended Greasing Intervals:

Standard Industrial Duty (Packaging/Conveyor):
  Interval: Every 500 operating hours or 6 months (whichever first)
  Duty: Ambient temperature -20°C to +60°C
  Product: MOVIGEAR® synthetic grease (SEW part no. 26.051.1000)

Heavy Duty (High-Speed or Shock Load):
  Interval: Every 250 operating hours or 3 months
  Duty: Continuous operation, high acceleration cycles
  Product: Same as standard

Temperature-Corrected Intervals:
  If ambient > 50°C: Reduce interval by 50% (use 250 hrs or 3 months)
  If ambient < 0°C: May extend to 12 months (verify low-temp grease compatibility)
  If ambient > 60°C: Consult SEW engineering (standard grease unsuitable)

===================================================
GREASING PROCEDURE
===================================================

Required Materials:
  - SEW synthetic grease (cartridge or pump dispenser)
  - Clean cloth (lint-free)
  - Instruction: Do NOT overfill

Steps:

1. Locate grease fitting on motor body
   (Usually marked "G" or "GREASE"—located on top or side)

2. Wipe fitting clean with cloth
   (Prevents dirt ingress)

3. Attach grease gun or pump to fitting

4. Pump grease slowly until:
   - Slight overflow appears at motor bearing cap, OR
   - Pressure gauge reads 4–6 bar (do NOT exceed)

5. Wipe excess grease immediately
   (Prevents contamination of nearby components)

6. Remove grease gun

7. Rotate shaft manually 2–3 full revolutions
   (Distributes fresh grease throughout bearing)

8. Record date and operator in maintenance log

WARNING: Overfilling causes overheating and seal leakage.

===================================================
BEARING REPLACEMENT
===================================================

Indicators for bearing replacement:
  - Audible grinding or rattling noise
  - Vibration at motor body (feel manually)
  - Temperature rise (>10°C above normal)
  - Visible grease leakage from bearing seal

Required tools:
  - Hex wrench set (for mounting bolts)
  - Bearing puller (if bearing is seized)
  - New bearing set (per motor model—see parts list)

Procedure:

1. Disconnect power and motor brake (electrical/mechanical)

2. Remove motor from mounting bracket
   - Note orientation (use alignment marks)
   - Document wire connections

3. Remove end bearing cap
   - Typically 4–6 bolts
   - Mark cap position (may have asymmetric bearing)

4. Extract bearing
   - If bearing rotates freely: pull straight out
   - If bearing is tight: use bearing puller
   - Do NOT force (may damage shaft)

5. Install new bearing
   - Clean shaft thoroughly
   - Press bearing onto shaft until seated
   - Bearing inner ring should spin freely

6. Replace bearing cap and seals
   - Apply thin layer of grease to seal surfaces
   - Tighten bolts evenly (prevent warping)
   - Torque specification: See motor label (typically 15–20 Nm)

7. Re-install motor on bracket using alignment marks

8. Reconnect electrical and brake wiring

9. Test rotation (manual low-speed before power)

===================================================
MOTOR COUPLING ALIGNMENT
===================================================

Misalignment causes excessive bearing wear and vibration.

Alignment Check Procedure:

1. Disengage motor brake and disconnect load

2. Measure runout using dial indicator:
   - Place indicator on coupling face
   - Rotate coupling 360°
   - Record maximum radial movement
   - Acceptable: <0.05 mm TIR (total indicated runout)

3. Check axial alignment (angular misalignment):
   - Mark motor and load shafts with reference lines
   - Measure gap at 0°, 90°, 180°, 270°
   - Acceptable: Variation <0.1 mm across circumference

4. If misaligned:
   - Loosen motor mounting bolts slightly
   - Adjust shim pack (thin metal shims between motor and bracket)
   - Verify alignment, then fully tighten bolts


Coupling Type: Flexible jaw or elastomer insert
  Check: Elastomer insert not cracked or deformed
  Replace if: Visible damage or excessive play between hubs

===================================================
THERMAL PROTECTION
===================================================

Maximum Operating Temperature:
  Motor winding: 100°C (Class F insulation)
  Bearing: 80°C (grease breakdown temperature)
  Housing: 60°C (touch-safe guidance)

Temperature Monitoring:
  - Embedded thermal protection switch (kills contactor if exceeded)
  - Manual reset required after thermal shutdown
  - Indicates: Overload, blocked load, or inadequate cooling

Cooling Recommendations:
  1. Ensure 150 mm clearance around motor for airflow
  2. Do NOT block cooling fins or vents
  3. In confined space: add forced cooling fan
  4. Ambient >50°C: verify duty cycle vs. motor rating

If frequent thermal shutdowns occur:
  - Verify load is not mechanically stuck
  - Check for jammed conveyor or binding
  - Reduce duty cycle (continuous run → intermittent)
  - Upgrade to higher-rated motor if load is permanent

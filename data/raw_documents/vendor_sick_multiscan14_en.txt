===================================================
MEASUREMENT PRINCIPLES
===================================================

The Multiscan14 uses time-of-flight (ToF) laser measurement to determine object
distance and dimensional consistency along a 1D line profile.

Key Specifications:
- Measurement Range: 50 mm to 2000 mm (typical)
- Repeatability: ±0.1 mm at 1000 mm distance (1σ)
- Measurement Frequency: 10 Hz, 25 Hz, or 50 Hz (configurable)
- Output: EtherCAT over industrial ethernet
- Wavelength: 905 nm (infrared)

===================================================
CALIBRATION PROCEDURE
===================================================

Required Materials:
- Calibration reference plate (included with unit)
- Mounting bracket specific to your application
- Reference distance measurement (gauge or caliper)

Steps:

1. Mount reference plate perpendicular to laser beam
   at distance specified in your configuration
   (usually 500-1000 mm from sensor)

2. Via engineering interface (Web UI or TIA Portal):
   Navigate to: Sensor Config → Calibration

3. Select "Run Calibration Routine"
   System will:
   - Take 10 measurements at current mounting position
   - Calculate zero-distance offset
   - Store calibration data in flash memory

4. Remove reference plate

5. Verify: Measure known reference object
   Confirm reading matches expected distance ±0.2 mm

===================================================
COMMON FAILURE MODES
===================================================

Problem: Measurement readings consistently too high/too low
Likely Cause: Calibration drift due to thermal change
Solution: Re-run calibration procedure (see Section 2)
Prevention: Calibrate once monthly or after 20°C temperature change

Problem: Erratic or missing readings
Likely Cause: Contaminated lens or optical misalignment
Solution: 
  1. Power down sensor
  2. Inspect front lens for dust, condensation
  3. Clean gently with soft, dry lens cloth
  4. Power on and test
Prevention: Protective lens cover when not in use

Problem: No EtherCAT communication
Likely Cause: Connector disconnection or cable damage
Solution:
  1. Verify M12 connector fully seated
  2. Check cable for damage (kinked, torn insulation)
  3. Power cycle sensor
  4. Verify sensor appears in EtherCAT network scan
Prevention: Cable strain relief, protected routing

Problem: Temperature warnings in status
Likely Cause: Sensor operating above 50°C ambient
Solution:
  - Verify mounting receives adequate air cooling
  - Check for reflected heat from nearby lights or heaters
  - Reduce measurement frequency if possible
Prevention: Install thermal barrier or ventilation

===================================================
ELECTRICAL INTERFACE
===================================================

Connector Type: M12-A 5-pin (female on sensor)

Pin Layout:
1. EtherCAT TX (P)
2. EtherCAT TX (N)
3. EtherCAT RX (P)
4. EtherCAT RX (N)
5. Shield

Cable Specifications:
- Type: CAT.6A industrial EtherCAT cable (as per IEC 61076-2-101)
- Maximum length: 100 meters per segment
- Minimum bend radius: 4× cable diameter
- Shielding: Must be connected to pin 5 and grounded at both ends

Power Supply:
- Via EtherCAT power coupler (SICK PLC branch connector)
- Typical draw: 500 mA at 24 VDC
- No separate power connection required

===================================================
DIAGNOSTIC INDICATORS
===================================================

LED Status (on sensor body):
- Green: Ready/Operating normally
- Orange: Warming up or in standby
- Red: Fault condition (check EtherCAT diagnostics)
- Off: No power

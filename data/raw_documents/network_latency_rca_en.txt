INDUSTRIAL ETHERNET LATENCY INVESTIGATION (RCA-NET-001)

Equipment: SCALANCE X312-3LD Managed Switch + Siemens S7-1500 EtherCAT Master
Affected Devices: Cognex InSight 7010, SICK Multiscan14, SEW Motor Controller x2
Date: 2026-03-17
Downtime Impact: No hard stop; 4.5 minutes of degraded throughput (cycle time +38%)

---
EVENT DESCRIPTION
---

During a product format changeover on Packaging Line 4, communication latency
between the S7-1500 PLC and all EtherCAT field devices increased significantly.
No hard production stop, but conveyor timing drifted causing product spacing
anomalies and an elevated vision false-reject rate (3.8% instead of <2%).

Primary symptom: EtherCAT cycle time rose from 10 ms baseline to 13.8 ms average
(+38%) over the 4.5-minute window.

Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 3: Threshold Alerts - Cycle Time >150% alert)

---
FINDINGS
---

Finding 1: Broadcast Traffic Spike During Recipe Download

  Root cause: Recipe download from HMI to Cognex InSight 7010 injects large
  UDP broadcast frames onto the EtherCAT network segment.

  Evidence:
  - SCALANCE X312 diagnostic showed broadcast traffic spike: 230 frames/sec
    during recipe download (normal: <10 frames/sec)
  - Correlated exactly with elevated cycle time window (11:34:20 - 11:38:50)
  - EtherCAT protocol is sensitive to broadcast interference (deterministic
    timing disrupted by non-EtherCAT traffic)

  Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 4: Latency Warnings - broadcast traffic)
  Reference: VENDOR-SIEMENS-S7-001 (Section 3: EtherCAT Communication Diagnostics)

Finding 2: SCALANCE Port 3 Intermittent CRC Errors

  Root cause: Port 3 (connected to SICK Multiscan14 laser sensor) showed
  recurring CRC frame errors due to marginal cable shielding.

  Evidence:
  - SCALANCE diagnostics: Port 3 showing 12 CRC errors per 1000 frames
    (threshold alert: >1% = >10 per 1000)
  - Cable visual inspection: Small nick in outer jacket at cable tray entry;
    partial shield breach visible
  - When SICK laser disconnected from Port 3, CRC errors ceased immediately

  Cable spec violation: Shield continuity measured at 8.7 Ohm (should be <5 Ohm)
  Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 5: Cable Testing - shield spec <5 Ohm)
  Reference: VENDOR-SICK-MS-001 (Section 3: Electrical Interface - shielding requirements)

Finding 3: Inconsistent Device Update Intervals

  Root cause: EtherCAT slave update intervals were not standardized at
  commissioning. Devices reporting at different intervals create irregular
  master cycle timing.

  Evidence:
  - TIA Portal EtherCAT configuration review:
    * SICK Multiscan14: Configured at 25 Hz (40 ms)
    * Cognex InSight 7010: Configured at 50 Hz (20 ms)
    * SEW Motor Controllers (x2): Configured at 10 Hz (100 ms)
  - Mixed update intervals force EtherCAT master to manage multiple timing
    windows simultaneously, reducing determinism
  - Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 3: EtherCAT Master Configuration)

---
ACTIONS TAKEN
---

1. Network Segmentation: Engineering traffic separated from EtherCAT runtime
   - SCALANCE VLAN configuration updated: VLAN 10 = EtherCAT only
     VLAN 20 = Engineering/HMI traffic
   - Recipe downloads from HMI now route through VLAN 20; no broadcast
     interference on VLAN 10
   - Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 4: Prevention - separate networks)

2. Faulty Cable Replacement:
   - SICK Multiscan14 EtherCAT cable replaced (full CAT.6A industrial cable)
   - New shield continuity: 0.9 Ohm (well within <5 Ohm spec)
   - Port 3 CRC error rate after replacement: 0.00% over 30-minute observation
   - Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 5: Cable Testing procedure)
   - Reference: VENDOR-SICK-MS-001 (Section 3: Cable spec requirements)

3. Update Interval Standardization:
   - All EtherCAT slaves reconfigured to 25 Hz (40 ms) as standard
   - Exceptions: SEW motor controllers must remain at 10 Hz (drive controller
     limitation - higher rates cause drive fault)
   - Result: Cycle time variance reduced from ±4.2 ms to ±0.8 ms
   - Reference: VENDOR-ECAT-NET-001 (Section 3: Slave parameter configuration)

4. SCALANCE Monitoring Alert Added:
   - Configured SCALANCE to alert Operations if Port CRC error rate >0.5%
   - Alert destination: WinCC alarm panel via SNMP trap
   - This enables earlier detection before latency reaches critical level

---
LESSONS LEARNED
---

1. EtherCAT and HMI/engineering traffic should always be on separate VLANs.
   Recipe downloads are frequent and bursty - do not share with real-time bus.

2. Cable shield integrity is a silent killer: partial shield damage causes
   intermittent CRC errors that are easy to miss without active monitoring.
   Visual inspection at commissioning AND after any cable routing changes.

3. Update interval standardization matters at commissioning, not after problems.
   Should be part of network configuration standard document.
